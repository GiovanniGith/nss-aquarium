Lion Fish



Size:

The Lionfish can grow to be 12-15 inches long.


Harvest Location:

North Carolina down to South America


Pricing:

The lionfish can cost anywhere between $15 to $110.


What it eats:

Indescriminate eaters, algae, other fish

Clown Fish



Size:

The clownfish usually grow to 4.3 inches.


Harvest Location:

Indonesia, Phillipines, Thailand, Australia, Maldives, Hawaii


Pricing:

The Clownfish can cost anywhere between $10 to $25, with larger ones costing up to $100


What it eats:

Clownfish eat various small invertebrates and algae, as well as food scraps the anemone leaves behind.

Electric Eel



Size:

Electric eels can grow to more than 8 feet in length and can weigh nearly 45 pounds.


Harvest Location:

Electric eels live in slow-moving freshwater creeks and swamps in the north-east of South America, including the Amazon and Orinoco basins.


Pricing:

An electric eel CAN COST AS LIST AS $15 AND UPWARDS OF $650 FOR LARGER ONES.


What it eats:

As adults, they eat amphibians, fishes and crustaceans.

Piranhas



Size:

Piranhas commonly grow to 12 inches in length and weigh less than two pounds, but have been reported to grow up to 16 inches and over 7 pounds.


Harvest Location:

Piranhas are native to the central and southern river systems of South America, where they inhabit tropical rivers and streams and are often found in murky water.


Pricing:

A piranha's price can vary widely from $5 to $650 or more depending on their species, size, and seller. On average, however, a piranha costs between $10 and $70. You can usually buy a group of 10 piranhas for around $100 to $200.


What it eats:

As opportunistic feeders, they eat fish, aquatic invertebrates, crustaceans, insects, algae and aquatic plants.
